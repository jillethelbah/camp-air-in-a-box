Session Delivery Options

How Will Sessions Be Delivered?

Choose between live facilitation by your own SMEs or pre-recorded sessions from the asynchronous library. You can mix both approaches within a single cohort.

SYNCHRONOUS
🎤
Have Your Own SME Deliver

Your subject matter experts deliver sessions live to participants. Best for interactive Q&A, team-specific customization, and real-time coaching.

  • Live facilitation with real-time interaction
  • Customizable to your team's context & tools
  • Uses structured facilitator lesson plans & session flows
  • Requires trained facilitator (TtT available)

📹

  • No live facilitator needed — ready to go

Choose Your Delivery Model

How Do You Want to Run Camp AIR?

Select the model that fits your scale. Each comes with a complete operational playbook — from registration to measurement.

👥
Team Model
10–30 people · 1 team/group

Run a focused Camp AIR experience for a single team. Ideal for managers who want to upskill their immediate team quickly with minimal coordination overhead.

  • Lightweight setup — manager-led coordination
  • Pre-built 3-week agenda & lab selection guide
  • Self-contained facilitator kit
  • Built-in measurement & feedback templates
🏢
Organization Model
50–200 people · Multiple teams

Coordinate Camp AIR across multiple teams within an org. Requires a program lead and light ops support for cohort management and scheduling.

  • Multi-cohort scheduling & registration
  • Facilitator train-the-trainer (TtT) program
  • Centralized comms & stakeholder reporting
  • Cross-team showcase & impact measurement
🌐
Division Model
200+ people · Division-wide

Scale Camp AIR across an entire division — as demonstrated by CoreAI (Dean & Aparna) and CAP (Thomas, Ronit & Jared). Full operational rigor required.

  • Enterprise registration & cohort orchestration
  • Master Trainer certification pipeline
  • Executive sponsor engagement model
  • Division-wide impact dashboard & ROI reporting

Operational Playbooks

What's Inside Each Model

Expand each section to see the details. Full playbooks with step-by-step guides are coming soon.

👥
Team Model — Manager-Led Delivery
10–30 participants · 3 weeks · Lightweight ops
▼

The Team Model is designed for a single manager to bring Camp AIR to their team with minimal overhead. The manager acts as the coordinator, using a pre-packaged facilitator kit and selecting from curated lab options.

  • Who runs it: Team manager + 1 facilitator (can be the same person)
  • Registration: Simple sign-up via Teams or email — no formal registration system needed
  • Labs: Pick 3–4 from the curated non-engineering or engineering lab catalog
  • Facilitation: Facilitator lesson plans included — no TtT required at this scale
  • Measurement: Pre/post survey templates + team impact summary
  • Timeline: 1 week to prep, 3 weeks to deliver, 1 week to wrap

📋 Detailed playbook with templates, timelines, and checklists — coming soon.

🏢
Organization Model — Program Lead-Driven
50–200 participants · Multiple cohorts · Coordinated ops
▼

The Org Model requires a dedicated program lead to coordinate across multiple teams. This model introduces cohort management, facilitator enablement (TtT), and structured comms.

  • Who runs it: Program lead + facilitator pool (2–5 facilitators)
  • Registration: Centralized registration form with cohort assignment
  • Labs: Consistent lab track across all cohorts with some customization
  • Facilitation: Train-the-Trainer session required — Master Trainer role activated
  • Measurement: Cross-cohort reporting dashboard + stakeholder readout
  • Comms: Pre-built email/Teams templates for announcements, reminders, wrap-ups
  • Timeline: 3 weeks to prep, 3 weeks to deliver, 2 weeks to measure & report

📋 Detailed playbook with templates, timelines, and checklists — coming soon.

🌐
Division Model — Enterprise-Scale Rollout
200+ participants · Division-wide · Full operational rigor
▼

The Division Model is the full enterprise playbook — battle-tested by CoreAI (Dean & Aparna) and CAP (Thomas, Ronit & Jared). This requires executive sponsorship, a dedicated ops team, and a certified facilitator pipeline.

  • Who runs it: Program manager + ops support + facilitator pipeline + exec sponsor
  • Registration: Enterprise registration system with wave/cohort management
  • Labs: Standardized lab catalog with governance for updates and versioning
  • Facilitation: Master Trainer certification + facilitator quality assurance
  • Measurement: Division-wide impact dashboard, ROI analysis, exec-level reporting
  • Content governance: Structured submission, review, approval, and retirement process
  • Comms: Full comms plan — exec kickoff, manager enablement, participant journey
  • Timeline: 4–6 weeks to prep, rolling 3-week cohorts, continuous measurement

📋 Detailed playbook with templates, timelines, and checklists — coming soon.
